Here we present an analysis of the skill of 8 seasonal forecasting systems in reproducing winter surface variables (air temperature, total precipitation and mean sea level pressure) in the Iberian Peninsula region.

Different verification metrics were used, both deterministic and probabilistic, all defined as in the WMO forecast guidance:

  • Spearman's rank correlation coefficient (Correlation): measures the time statistical dependence between the forecast ensemble mean and the observations.
  • Area under the relative (or 'receiver') operating characteristic (ROC) curve: this score measures the ability of the forecast to discriminate between the terciles of a given variable, with higher values indicating a good discrimination for a specific tercile. It is calculated for each category or tercile.
  • Ranked probability skill score (RPSS): this score is meant as a summary of the forecast ability for verifying all the considered categories. Positive values indicate a better tercile discrimination of the forecast than that of the climatology.

Variables definition:

  • t2m (IP): 2m air temperature in the Iberian Peninsula [9.5°W-3.5°E and 36°N-44°N].
  • t2m (CI): 2m air temperature in the Canary Islands region [18.5°W-13°W and 27.5°N-29.5°N].
  • tp (IP): total precipitation in the Iberian Peninsula [9.5°W-3.5°E and 36°N-44°N].
  • tp (CI): total precipitation in the Canary Islands region [18.5°W-13°W and 27.5°N-29.5°N].
  • msl: mean sea level pressure in the [30°W-5°E and 20°N-50°N] region.
