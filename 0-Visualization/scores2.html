Modes of variability

Back

Here we present an analysis of the skill of 8 seasonal forecasting systems in reproducing four different European modes of variability: the North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO), the East Atlantic (EA), the East Atlantic/Western Russia (EAWR) and the Scandinavian pattern (SCA).

Different verification metrics were used, both deterministic and probabilistic, all defined as in the WMO forecast guidance:

  • Spearman's rank correlation coefficient (Correlation): measures the time statistical dependence between the forecast ensemble mean and the observations.
  • Area under the relative (or 'receiver') operating characteristic (ROC) curve: this score measures the ability of the forecast to discriminate between the terciles of a given variable, with higher values indicating a good discrimination for a specific tercile. It is calculated for each category or tercile.
  • Ranked probability skill score (RPSS): this score is meant as a summary of the forecast ability for verifying all the considered categories. Positive values indicate a better tercile discrimination of the forecast than that of the climatology.

Variables definition:

  • NAO (box): North Atlantic Oscillaion calculated as the difference between the normalized anomaly of sea level pressure between a South-Atlantic and a North-Atlantic lat-lon box. The methodology is based on Baker et al., 2018.
  • NAO (eof): North Atlantic Oscillaion calculated with an Emprirical Orthogonal Function analysis. The methodology is based on Lledó et al., 2020.
  • EA: East Atlantic calculated with EOFs.
  • EAWR: East Atlantic / Western Russia calculated with EOFs.
  • SCA: Scandinavian Pattern calculated with EOFs.

Baker, L. H., Shaffrey, L. C., Sutton, R. T., Weisheimer, A., & Scaife, A. A. (2018). An intercomparison of skill and overconfidence/underconfidence of the wintertime North Atlantic Oscillation in multimodel seasonal forecasts. Geophysical Research Letters, 45(15), 7808-7817.

Lledó, L., Cionni, I., Torralba, V., Bretonniere, P. A., & Samso, M. (2020). Seasonal prediction of Euro-Atlantic teleconnections from multiple systems. Environmental Research Letters, 15(7), 074009.
